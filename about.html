Hey! I'm Sean, a digital designer based in Toronto. My goal is to design products and interfaces that are beautiful to look at and add value to people's lives.

Currently, I'm in my final year of the Bachelor of Design program at York University, and work part-time as a designer for Student Recruitment and Admissions. This past summer, I had the pleasure of interning as an Associate Designer at EY Canada within their design studio practice. My primary area of focus is in the sphere of UI/UX design, though I enjoy incorporating visual identity and motion into my work whenever possible. I am familiar with industry-standard software including the Adobe suite of apps, Figma, Webflow, and basic front-end development.

I believe the future of digital design must be one that takes a holistic approach to people. This means designing with ethical standards and crafting experiences that treat users as people, not products. It means design that enhances well-being, solves critical problems, and respects autonomy and privacy. It also means interfaces that are inclusive, where accessibility is foundational. These principles guide the work I do as an interaction designer.

In my free time, you'll find me listening to the Vergecast or reading about technology, browsing my favourite design studios, working on side-projects, and watching my favourite shows on Netflix. If you have an opportunity or just want to chat, feel free to reach out!

Simplicity

My style is minimalist. I strive to create intuitive interfaces by decluttering pages and presenting content methodically. Where appropriate, I take stylistic direction from Apple and Google developer guidelines.

Scalability

Flexible design systems are central to my work. This means systems that are designed to scale over time, and interfaces that look beautiful and function equally on every device size.

Accessibility

Creating inclusive experiences means designing for a wide range of end users. I am AODA certified, and aim to incorporate web accessibility best practices into the foundation of every project.

Integrity

Most critically, I design products that are ethical; interfaces that treat users with autonomy and respect. My goal is to design truly useful experiences that add value to people's lives and their communities.
